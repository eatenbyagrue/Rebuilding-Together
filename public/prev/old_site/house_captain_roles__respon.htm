The House Captain Roles & Responsibilities are a crucial element in making the Rebuilding Together program a success. The skills, hard work and dedication of the House Captains gets the repair and rehabilitation work completed effectively and efficiently. This role is challenging and at the same time very rewarding.

 The following dates and forms are important to the House Captain.

  • March 6, 2008 – House Captain Orientation Meeting is critical for learning new procedures regarding lists and time lines. The House Captain Handbook for Planning Ahead will help you prepare for the big day.   House Captains select houses at this meeting.

  • March 24, 2008 – Homeowner Inspection completed by this date to define a Scope of Work and have the homeowner sign the Homeowner Agreement.

  • April 4, 2008 – Volunteer Estimate Form and General Materials List Form must be turned in by this date.  We need to know the number of skilled and unskilled volunteers you need.  It is extremely important to get your material lists to us on time.  Be sure to employ safe work practices including Lead-Safe Procedures.

  • April 19, 2008 – Prep day is scheduled to accommodate large jobs where more than one stage of work needs to take place, or where power washing and prepping need to be done prior to the 28th.

  • April 24-25, 2008 – Materials pick-up 12-8pm from the University of Portland Maintenance Plant building.

  • April 26, 2008 – Portland’s Big workday!  The Workday Handbook clarifies details to ensure a good day for everyone.  All volunteers must sign the Volunteer Agreement form and minors need to have a parent/guardian complete the Authorization for Participating Minors form before working on the project.  Those waiver forms will be included in the House Captain package when you pick up materials.  Use the Volunteer List Worksheet to take role on the big day.  Big projects with a lot of volunteers should assign a House Liaison person and read the Guidelines for House Liaisons. 

  • May 2, 2008 – House Captain Evaluation Form is due back at head quarters. House Captains must communicate whether or not all of the work stared was completed. If it has not, please confirm what arrangements have been made for completion.

All during the months of March and April, the House Captain is the key person coordinating his/her specific project to make sure it is a good day for the volunteers and homeowner.  That includes communications with volunteers and the Rebuilding Together Portland office to ensure the homeowner, volunteers, materials, supplies and support teams are staged and ready to rock-and-roll on the big day.  The House Captain is supported by the Rebuilding Together office and support volunteers to ensure the day goes smoothly.  Rebuilding Together Portland has successfully completed more than 800 projects since 1990, so you can be assured that experienced planning support stands behind you as a House Captain.   

We continue to look for additional people to fill the role of House Captain, if you know of someone qualified and interested please ask them to call our office to get involved this year.  In addition, we are always looking for qualified people to screen the 150 homeowners wishing to become Rebuilding Together Portland beneficiaries.  If you re interested open this Homeowner Interviewer House Inspection Sign-up Form

If you are a House Captain, thank you for making Rebuilding Together such a great project for the homeowner, volunteers and your community.
