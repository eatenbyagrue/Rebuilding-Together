FREE HOME REPAIRS …available for low-income homeowners

Rebuilding Together Portland is the leading local volunteer organization that, in partnership with the community, rehabilitates the homes of low-income homeowners, particularly the elderly, disabled, and families with children so that they can continue to live in warmth, safety, and independence. Rebuilding Together Portland also assists in the rehabilitation of community centers, schools and non-profits in Portland. We unite people of all faiths, walks of life, racial and ethnic groups, young and seasoned, rich and poor in an effort to assist people in need. We are a non-partisan, non-sectarian, and not-for-profit. The Portland chapter was established in 1990 and has been growing steadily each year both in the number of buildings rehabilitated and the number of volunteers.

Originally started in 1973 in Midland, TX, Rebuilding Together is now active in all 50 States and serves over 625 towns and communities. Rebuilding Together began as “Christmas in April” in 1973, when volunteers in Midland, Texas decided to devote one day in April to repairing and sprucing up the dilapidated home of low-income, disabled, and elderly neighbors in their community. The idea spread from town to town and city to city across the country. By 1982, the program had reached the Washington, DC area (where volunteers included Cabinet members, White House staff and other national figures). With a commitment to spread the program to every state, Christmas in April*USA was launched in 1988. In 2003, the name was changed to Rebuilding Together to broadening mission year round.

Each year Rebuilding Together-Portland, screens about 150 low income homeowners and selects about 50-60 homes for repair. In addition 5-8 non-profit organizations benefit from projects throughout the year. Rebuilding Together-Portland organizes 1,200-1,500 volunteers from all walks of life to unite on the last Saturday in April each year to complete equivalent to $900,000 worth of improvements to the community on that day. In addition, smaller projects are performed throughout the year. Since 1990, the Portland chapter has utilized the energies of 15,500 volunteers to fix up over 800 homes of low income, disabled, families with children and elderly homeowners mostly in Portland.

The success of Rebuilding Together Portland is a result of the support received from sponsors and volunteers.

Homeowner safety and security are the first priorities of Rebuilding Together. Our skilled and unskilled volunteers do all types of work including painting, plumbing, electrical, carpentry, roofing, weatherization, yard cleaning, debris removal, and much more!
